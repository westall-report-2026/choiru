2. Vision, Mission, and Strategic Focus

Foundation

2.1 Vision (2025–2027)

To develop Masjid Westall as a spiritually enriching, inclusive, and structured community hub that nurtures iman, strengthens ukhuwah, and empowers every individual to grow and contribute.

Masjid Westall serves as:

  • A place of worship (Objective 1)
  • A centre of learning: Islamic and General Teaching (Objective 2)
  • A hub for community connection (Objective 3)

Focus Area: Strengthening Community Activities

Mission 1

Promote program-based leadership rooted in iman, ukhuwah, and amal.

Mission 2

Empower structured involvement with clear roles and ownership.

Mission 3

Strengthen iman through strategic religious programs and learning.

Mission 4

Elevate youth and community engagement through meaningful initiatives.

Mission 5

Foster ukhuwah across seniors, new members, families, and younger generations.

9. Reflection & Journey 2025

Narrative Timeline

9.1 Foundation Phase – Strengthening Religious Core

The 2025 journey began with a strong emphasis on establishing the religious foundation of Masjid Westall, primarily through the Religious & Dakwah Division (Kerohanian).

  • Hadith sessions after Subuh prayer
  • Expansion of regular kajian and literacy programs
  • Appointment of Ustadz Umair as Imam
  • Early establishment of Sister Mashael, Pak Widodo, and Rizka
  • Emergence of young imams and hafiz
  • Early regeneration pipeline alongside Ustadz Umair

9.2 Program Establishment – Strong Base, Untapped Potential

By mid-2025, programs under the Religious & Dakwah Division were successfully initiated, structurally organised, and supported by available resources, though there remains significant room for expansion.

9.3 Second Phase – Fundraising Shift (Q2 Focus)

A major milestone led by Muslimah Westall and Mbak Erin brought a landmark fundraising event with Ustadz Donny Rais, with AUD 140,000 pledged and AUD 115,000 successfully collected.

9.4 Third Phase – Media, Communication & Campaign Support

Media functions and website initiatives expanded to strengthen events promotion, fundraising campaigns, and community engagement, especially during financial pressure before Idul Fitri.

9.5 Youth Engagement & Regeneration

Beyond Madrasah Westall (Group Cinta), youth involvement expanded into operational support, event participation, and religious and leadership development.

9.6 Facility & Education Utilisation Gap

A key reflection is the gap between infrastructure readiness and program utilisation. Classroom utilisation is not yet optimal, and the education ecosystem of Madrasah Westall, YIMSA, and MIIS is not yet fully integrated.

9.7 Stakeholder Continuity & Leadership Momentum

Muslimah Westall and Youth Westall (under Fikrie’s coordination) should continue to be empowered and supported as key drivers of sustainability.

9.8 Key Gaps Identified

  • Limited integration between Madrasah Westall, YIMSA, and MIIS
  • Underutilisation of education infrastructure
  • Need for more structured expansion of existing programs

9.9 Forward Reflection – Direction Beyond 2025

  • New Place: expansion or acquisition of a larger, more sustainable space
  • New Business Model: income-generating initiatives and reduced dependency on event-based fundraising
  • Grants & External Funding: government and institutional grants for stronger sustainability

10 Personal Reflection & Family Acknowledgement

Personal Note

I would like to take this opportunity to share a personal reflection openly and sincerely with the community.

The decision to step down from my role as Takmir is not something that happened suddenly. It has been the result of a long process of reflection and consideration. Over time, I came to realise that I have not been able to carry out the role as the main executor as well as I had intended.

By nature, I tend to go all out and seek full control in what I do. While this has helped in initiating many programs, I have come to understand that this approach has also had unintended consequences—particularly on my own wellbeing.

In recent months, I experienced moments where I felt overwhelmed and struggled to keep up with the demands of responsibilities. There were times when I was physically unwell, yet continued to push through. While the amanah continued to run, I began to feel the impact not only on my health but also on other aspects of my life, including my academic responsibilities. There were even instances where I did not perform as expected academically because I placed those responsibilities as the last priority.

These are realities that, for a long time, I did not fully acknowledge.

I also want to emphasise that my wife has been the person who supported me the most throughout this journey. Her support has never been a limitation, but rather a source of strength. Precisely because of her care and concern, she was the first to notice the changes in my condition—often earlier than I realised myself. As the closest person to me, she has seen the full picture of my situation, while I, as the one going through it, often overlooked it.

I would also like to express my sincere gratitude to my children, Fathan and Ghayda.

Although they may not fully understand the responsibilities I was carrying, they have been part of this journey in their own way—through their patience, their understanding when I was not fully present, and their quiet support at home. There were many moments when my time and attention were divided, and I realise that this has also affected them.

Their presence has been a reminder for me to reflect more deeply on balance, responsibility, and what truly matters. For that, I am deeply grateful and ask for forgiveness for the moments I could have been more present as a father.

The resign intention shared earlier, before Ramadhan 2026, with Pak Aru and Pak Ade. However, I was waiting for the right time to formally communicate this decision.

I also recognise that I am not someone who can carry amanah in a minimal or partial way. I tend to initiate many things—across media, kerohanian, youth, grants, and other areas—which eventually created an imbalance, not only within myself but also affecting those closest to me, especially my family. In this, I acknowledge that I have not managed myself well.

For these reasons, I believe that stepping back at this point is necessary for me to recover, recalibrate, and improve my personal condition.
