Vision

2025–2027

To develop Masjid Westall as a spiritually enriching, inclusive, and structured community hub that nurtures iman, strengthens ukhuwah, and empowers every individual to grow and contribute.

Masjid Westall aims to serve all levels of the community — from children to elders — not only as a place of worship, but also as a center for education, connection, and personal development.

Core Roles

  • A place of worship
  • A centre of learning: Islamic and general teaching
  • A hub for community connection

Mission 5

Key Financial Challenges & Direction

Masjid Westall remains partly dependent on event-based fundraising spikes. Long-term sustainability will require stronger recurring income models, including wakaf systems and monthly donor programs.

Transition & Future Direction (2026)

A shift from activities to systems, from events to measurable impact, and from donations to sustainability.

Education Expansion

Strengthen collaboration with Madrasah Westall, activate classroom use, and expand skill-based programs such as technology, tutoring, and youth-focused education.

Youth Engagement

Engage second-generation youth through regeneration pathways, leadership opportunities, and partnerships with student organisations such as YIMSA and MIIS.

Westall Hub

Develop a digital platform for bookings, program calendars, masjid history, updates, documentation, and broader community communication.

Shift 1
Activities → Systems
Build structured processes, clearer workflows, and sustainable operating models.
Shift 2
Events → Measurable Impact
Move toward clearer outcomes, participation tracking, and program accountability.
Shift 3
Donations → Sustainability
Support long-term planning through recurring support and better financial alignment.
